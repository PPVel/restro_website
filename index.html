Shrimp Tempura Sushi Bowls

The term tempura is a Japanese word used to refer to seafood or vegetables prepared by dipping them in a batter and deep frying them in hot oil. Tempura shrimp is a delectable dish in Japanese cuisine made by frying sweet and savoury shrimp with crispy batter and crumbs..

Tempura shrimp is often served as an appetizer. It is made by coating unshelled shrimp in a light batter made of wheat flour, eggs and liquid. The batter coated shrimp is deep fried and the tempura batter becomes crunchy and light with the juicy shrimp on the inside. Tempura is a cream color and lighter than many other fried foods. Tempura shrimp tastes sweet and gently nutty. It is served hot.

Hokkaido Scallops Panta Bhat

A table side preparation of the humble fermented rice in torani along with smoked Hokkaido Scallops in Kashmiri chili topped with many accompaniments is a true culinary revelation.

One of the distinguishing features of Hokkaido scallops is their impressive size and meatiness. These scallops are known for their large, succulent meat, making each bite a sumptuous and satisfying affair. The substantial size of the scallops allows for a variety of cooking techniques, from pan-searing to grilling, without compromising their tenderness or succulence. Their generous portions make them a perfect choice for those seeking a memorable and visually impressive seafood centerpiece at their dining table.
